Praktica M50 BF data

Specifications


  • Production years:
    1990 (mine bought in 2006)
  • Camera type:
    Motorized film camera
  • Lens:
    PRAKTICAR 4.5/29mm, 3 groups hybrid lens
  • Focus:
    Fixed focus, 1.5m to ∞
  • Aperture:
    f/4.5
  • Shutter speed:
    1/250 sec
  • Film type:
    35mm (ISO 100/200 and 400)
  • Light meter:
    None
  • Viewfinder:
    Big finder with Albada-bright frame. Magnification: 0.66x. Picture area: over 80%
  • Film winding:
    Automatic winding and power rewinding
  • Power source:
    2 AA alkaline batteries. (NiCd batteries cannot be used.)
  • Flash:
    Built-in flash. Can be manually turned on or off. Includes red-eye reduction. Recycling time: approx. 8 sec.

Facts


  • I found this camera when I was visiting my grandma.
    Turns out, it used to belong to my uncle
    . I asked my aunt if I could borrow it — she said yes, it had just been sitting in a box. She was happy that someone would finally use it!
  • In the box,
    I found two cameras: this one and an Olympus.
    The Olympus was quite similar to the Skina (page in work) I already had. I chose the Praktica because
    it has red-eye reduction and a data imprint feature
    — it prints the date on the photos! So I borrowed it.
  • When I got home, I put in two AA batteries and found that the camera worked perfectly — except for the data print. The small screen for the data didn't work. I was scared it was broken, but then I noticed some screws. After opening it, I found a CR2025 battery inside.
    Turns out, the data module has its own power source! After replacing it, the screen works again.
    I haven't tested it with film yet, though. (Update! It works!)
  • The element which pushing film was kinda broken
    , but i'm glued it. It steel needs careful use. You need to smoothly close the camera, when switching the film
